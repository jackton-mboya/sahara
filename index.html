Sahara

The Sahara Desert, spanning North Africa, is the world's largest hot desert, covering over 9 million square kilometers. Characterized by scorching temperatures, vast sand dunes, and arid landscapes, it receives minimal rainfall. Despite its harsh conditions, the Sahara hosts unique flora, fauna, and ancient cultural treasures, such as rock art and archaeological sites.
